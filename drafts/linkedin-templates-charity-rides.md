# LinkedIn templates — annual charity rides

Reusable post templates for the two annual fundraising rides (ADA Tour de Cure and MS Waves to Wine). Fill in the bracketed fields each year, attach a ride photo or short clip, and post during ride week.

**Posting cadence:** one post per ride, ideally the day after you finish (highest engagement). One follow-up post toward the end of the donation window if you're close to the team goal — short, urgency-framed.

**Why two templates, not one:** the two causes have different audiences and different framings. ADA → diabetes is broader-touching, more universal. MS → MS is more specialized but the National MS Society fundraising community is highly mobilized and rewards specificity. Don't merge them.

---

## ADA Tour de Cure — annual template

```
Back on the bike for the American Diabetes Association's Tour de Cure this year.

Diabetes affects more than 38 million Americans, and the ADA's research, advocacy, and direct patient-support work is some of the highest-leverage charitable work in U.S. healthcare. I ride every year because the people closest to me have been touched by it, and because the money raised actually moves the needle on prevention research and care access.

[YEAR]'s ride: [MILES] miles through [COURSE / LOCATION], finishing with $[AMOUNT] raised toward this year's team goal of $[GOAL].

Donations open through [END DATE]: [YOUR PERSONAL FUNDRAISING URL]

Every dollar goes directly to the ADA. Sharing this post — or pushing it to anyone in your network who'd care — means as much as a donation.

#TourdeCure #DiabetesResearch #ADA
```

**Fields to fill in each year:**
- `[YEAR]` — e.g., 2026
- `[MILES]` — e.g., 62 (metric century is common; pick your distance)
- `[COURSE / LOCATION]` — e.g., "the rolling Napa Valley course" or "the Silicon Valley loop"
- `[AMOUNT]` — current raised total at time of posting
- `[GOAL]` — your personal or team goal
- `[END DATE]` — when the donation page closes (usually 2–6 weeks post-ride)
- `[YOUR PERSONAL FUNDRAISING URL]` — link from your ADA participant page

**Optional add-ons:**
- Attach 1–2 photos (you on the bike at a landmark, finish line)
- Or attach a short clip from the ride (mid-ride landscape works better than face-to-camera)
- Tag riding partners or your team captain

## MS Waves to Wine — annual template

```
Suited up again for the National MS Society's Waves to Wine ride this year.

Multiple sclerosis affects nearly 1 million Americans, and the National MS Society funds the bulk of U.S. MS research, advocates for healthcare access for patients, and runs direct programs for people living with the disease. Waves to Wine is one of their largest annual fundraisers — a two-day ride through Sonoma's wine country, with every dollar going to MS research and patient support.

[YEAR]'s ride: [MILES] miles, [WEATHER NOTE], [HIGHLIGHT OR LOWLIGHT — a hill, a stretch, a tailwind]. Finished with $[AMOUNT] raised toward this year's team goal of $[GOAL].

Donations open through [END DATE]: [YOUR PERSONAL FUNDRAISING URL]

100% of donations go to the National MS Society. If you've been thinking about giving to MS work this year, this is a good window to do it.

#WavestoWine #BikeMS #MultipleSclerosis #NationalMSSociety
```

**Fields to fill in each year:**
- `[YEAR]` — e.g., 2026
- `[MILES]` — your day-one or two-day total
- `[WEATHER NOTE]` — "in unseasonal heat", "with a real headwind on Day Two", "perfect cool fog through Marin", etc.
- `[HIGHLIGHT OR LOWLIGHT]` — one specific moment, course feature, or near-failure that humanizes the post (NOT a generic "great ride")
- `[AMOUNT]` — current raised total at time of posting
- `[GOAL]` — your personal or team goal
- `[END DATE]` — when the donation page closes
- `[YOUR PERSONAL FUNDRAISING URL]` — link from your National MS Society participant page

**Optional add-ons:**
- Photo at the Day-One finish or overnight stop (Rohnert Park area is the typical anchor)
- Short clip of the Marin Headlands climb if you got one
- Tag your captain or team

## Optional follow-up post (3–5 days before donation window closes)

Use only if you're within striking distance of your goal and need a final push. Keep it short — long posts at this stage drag down engagement.

```
Three days left to donate to my [RIDE NAME] page. [$X to go / [N]% to goal] — if you've been meaning to and haven't, this is the window: [URL]

100% to [ADA / National MS Society].
```

## Notes on use

- **Don't post both rides in the same week** — they cannibalize each other's engagement on the same audience.
- **Native video > YouTube link.** If you have ride footage, upload the .mp4 directly to LinkedIn. The algorithm strongly prefers native video.
- **Photos of you on the bike outperform photos of the landscape.** People donate to people, not to scenery.
- **The "why this cause" paragraph stays constant year over year.** That consistency is good — it builds your association with the cause over time. Only the tactical/year-specific paragraphs should change.
- **Resharing your ride post by tagging a single specific person who you know cares about the cause (rather than a generic "share if you can") consistently produces 2–3x the reach.**
- **For an audience that already knows you ride annually**, the second-year post can drop some of the framing and lean harder into the year-over-year story ("Year 5 of the Tour de Cure for me, and the team has now raised $X total across five rides — here's what changes when you keep showing up").
